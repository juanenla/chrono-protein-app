Phase 01

CATABOLIC
WINDOW

Duration 0–30 min post-exercise

Your body is still in emergency mode — burning and breaking down. Without action, it starts dismantling muscle proteins for energy.

  • 📈 Cortisol spikes: The stress hormone is at its post-exercise peak — it signals the body to break down muscle protein into amino acids for fuel.
  • ⚡ Glycogen depleted: Muscle carbohydrate stores are empty. The body turns to protein (gluconeogenesis) to maintain blood glucose.
  • 🚨 Muscle protein breakdown (MPB) > MPS: Net protein balance is negative. You are losing muscle on net until you eat.
  • 🔓 GLUT4 transporters exposed: Muscle cell membranes are maximally permeable to glucose — the best insulin sensitivity of the day.
🌱
Phase 02

ANABOLIC
WINDOW

Duration 30 min–6 h post-exercise

Once you feed, the body pivots to building. This is the window where the work you did in the gym becomes actual new muscle fibers.

  • 🏗️ mTORC1 activated: Leucine triggers this master switch — it activates ribosomes to start building new muscle proteins (MPS > MPB).
  • 🍬 Glycogen resynthesis: Muscles absorb glucose 2–3× faster than normal. Carbohydrates eaten now refuel for tomorrow's workout efficiently.
  • 📉 Cortisol suppressed by insulin: Protein + carbs elevate insulin, which directly counters cortisol's muscle-breakdown signal.
  • 🔧 Satellite cells activated: Muscle stem cells migrate to micro-damage sites — the actual repair and growth process begins here.
THE METABOLIC CASCADE

What happens inside your body, step by step, from final rep to full recovery

💥
Exercise ends — mechanical damage + energy depletion 0 min

Myofibrils (muscle protein fibers) have micro-tears. ATP is depleted. Glycogen stores are 40–80% empty. Lactic acid elevated. This damage is intentional — it triggers adaptation.

📊
Cortisol peaks + catecholamines still elevated 0–15 min

Cortisol activates muscle protein breakdown (MPB) — breaking down contractile proteins (actin, myosin) into free amino acids. Adrenaline/noradrenaline still elevated, keeping the body in catabolic "fight" mode.

🔁
Nutrient-sensing: the body "asks" if food is available 15–45 min

AMPK (energy sensor) is elevated — it detects low glycogen and low amino acid availability. If no food arrives, AMPK suppresses mTOR and inhibits protein synthesis. If food arrives, AMPK falls and mTOR can fire.

🚀
Leucine arrives → mTORC1 fires → ribosomes activated 30–90 min (post meal)

Leucine (~2–3g minimum) directly binds SESN2/Rag GTPases, translocating mTORC1 to the lysosomal surface where it activates. This phosphorylates p70S6K and 4E-BP1 — the switches that tell ribosomes to start building contractile proteins.

🏗️
Net protein balance flips positive — MPS > MPB 1–4 hours

Muscle protein synthesis (MPS) rate elevates 50–100% above baseline. The amino acids from food — not from muscle breakdown — are now the building material. Simultaneously, insulin suppresses further cortisol signaling. This is real muscle growth.

🌙
Overnight: GH pulses drive sustained repair Sleep (6–8 h)

70% of daily Growth Hormone is released in the first 2 hours of deep sleep — it stimulates IGF-1, which independently activates protein synthesis and satellite cell proliferation. Pre-sleep casein provides a sustained 7-hour amino acid supply to fuel this nocturnal anabolism.

POST-WORKOUT ACTION PROTOCOL

Exactly what to do and eat, and when — in the right order

⏱️
Step 1
Immediate (0–30 min)
Urgent
Fast carbs first: 30–60g simple carbs (banana, rice cakes, sports drink). Raises insulin → stops cortisol → refills glycogen fast.
Water + electrolytes: 500 ml minimum. Dehydration by 2% reduces MPS by 15%.
Skip this step if you trained fasted — then add protein immediately too.
🍗
Step 2
Post-Workout Meal (30–90 min)
Critical
Protein: 25–40g with ≥3g leucine. Best sources: whey, eggs, chicken, fish.
Carbs: 40–80g complex — rice, oats, sweet potato. Maximizes glycogen resynthesis while insulin still elevated.
Fat: keep low (<15g) here — fat slows amino acid absorption, dulling the anabolic spike.
🕐
Step 3
Next Meal (3–4 hours later)
Important
Another 20–35g protein — MPS cycle resets. Don't wait >5 hours or you lose the second anabolic spike.
Complex carbs + vegetables — sustained energy and anti-inflammatory micronutrients (omega-3, antioxidants support recovery).
This is where chronotype matters: evening athletes should make this their largest meal.
🌙
Step 4
Pre-Sleep (30 min before bed)
Non-negotiable
30–40g CASEIN protein — slow-digesting, sustains amino acids for 7 hours during sleep. Cottage cheese, micellar casein powder, Greek yogurt.
Fuels GH-driven repair — 70% of daily Growth Hormone pulses in first 2 hours of deep sleep.
Evidence: +22% overnight MPS (Res 2012), +7.8% muscle mass over 12 weeks (Snijders 2015).
MYTHS VS REALITY

The most common post-workout nutrition mistakes — corrected by evidence

❌ Myth

"You must eat protein within 30 minutes or you lose your gains." The anabolic window is 30 minutes wide.

✅ Reality

The window is 4–6 hours total around training. If you ate 2h before training, you have 4h after. Only if fasted does the 30-min rule apply. (Aragon & Schoenfeld, 2013)

❌ Myth

"More protein = more muscle." Eating 90g in one meal is better than 3×30g.

✅ Reality

MPS saturates at ~0.4 g/kg per meal (~28g for 70 kg). Excess amino acids are oxidized for energy, not used to build muscle. Spread protein across 4 meals. (Areta 2013, Morton 2018)

❌ Myth

"Carbs after training make you fat." Avoid carbs post-workout.

✅ Reality

Post-exercise carbs go directly to muscle glycogen, not fat cells. Insulin sensitivity is at its peak — this is the safest time to eat carbohydrates. (Morris 2015, Saad 2012)

❌ Myth

"Eating before bed makes you gain fat." Never eat protein at night.

✅ Reality

Pre-sleep casein increases overnight muscle protein synthesis by 22% with no fat storage — the amino acids are used for repair, not stored. (Res 2012, Snijders 2015)

⚠️ Evidence note for app users: The post-exercise protein + carb protocol (Steps 1–3) is backed by >50 human RCTs with HIGH confidence. The pre-sleep casein recommendation has multiple dedicated 12-week RCTs (HIGH confidence). The chronotype-specific timing of these windows is evidence-informed extrapolation from circadian biology + performance research — not yet directly tested as a combined protocol. Present as "optimized scheduling," not "proven individualized prescription."
